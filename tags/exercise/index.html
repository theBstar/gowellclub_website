Exercise is vital for seniors as it promotes physical health, enhances mobility, and helps prevent chronic diseases such as heart disease and diabetes. Regular physical activity strengthens muscles and bones, improves balance and coordination, and reduces the risk of falls, fostering greater independence. Additionally, exercise is linked to improved mental health, reducing symptoms of anxiety and depression while enhancing cognitive function. Engaging in social activities like group classes can also foster connections and combat loneliness. Overall, incorporating exercise into daily routines not only boosts longevity but also enriches the quality of life for seniors.
